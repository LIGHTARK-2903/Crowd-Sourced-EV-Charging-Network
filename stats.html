Statistical Report

India's EV market is projected to reach 10 million units by 2030, driven by government policies and rising environmental awareness. Carbon-neutral EV charging is critical to ensuring the sustainability of India's transportation electrification goals.



Renewable Energy Potential in India


As of 2024, India's renewable energy capacity stands at approximately 175 GW, with:


⚪️ Solar energy : 70 GW

⚪️ Wind energy : 45 GW

⚪️ Hydropower : 47 GW

⚪️ Biomass energy : 10 GW


India aims to achieve 500 GW of renewable energy capacity by 2030, providing ample potential for integrating renewable energy into EV charging.



EV Charging Infrastructure in India


⚪️ The number of EV charging stations increased from 1,000 in 2020 to over 7,000 in 2024.

⚪️ By 2030, India targets the deployment of over 100,000 charging stations.

⚪️ Renewable energy-powered charging stations currently represent 10-15% of the total.



Carbon Emissions from EV Charging


A conventional EV charged using grid electricity contributes an average of 0.8 kg CO2 per kWh, depending on India's coal-heavy energy mix.

Transitioning to carbon-neutral charging can reduce this figure to near zero, supporting India's net-zero emissions goal by 2070.



Current Initiatives for Carbon-Neutral EV Charging


1. Public-Private Partnerships :

⚪️ Companies like Tata Power and EESL are deploying solar-powered EV charging stations.

⚪️ Ola Electric has set up 50 solar charging hubs across India.


2. Carbon Offsetting Projects :

India is part of the global carbon credit market, with projects focusing on reforestation and renewable energy contributing to offsets.


3. Pilot Projects :

Maharashtra and Delhi have initiated renewable energy-based EV charging stations.



Challenges in Achieving Carbon-Neutral EV Charging


1. Grid Dependence : Over 60% of India's electricity comes from coal, making full renewable integration challenging.

2. High Initial Costs : Setting up renewable energy-powered charging stations is 20-30% more expensive than conventional setups.

3. Energy Storage Limitations : The adoption of battery storage systems remains limited due to high costs and scalability issues.



Benefits for Stakeholders


For Users :

⚪️ Potential cost savings : Charging during off-peak hours with renewable energy can reduce costs by 20-30%.

⚪️ Reduction in carbon footprint by 90-100% when using fully renewable-powered chargers.


For India :

⚪️ Reduction in dependence on fossil fuels.

⚪️ Contribution to international commitments under the Paris Agreement.



Recommendations for Scaling Carbon-Neutral Charging


⚪️ Policy Incentives: Introduce subsidies and tax benefits for renewable energy-powered EV charging infrastructure.

⚪️ Investment in R&D: Develop cost-effective energy storage solutions to complement renewable energy.

⚪️ Awareness Campaigns: Educate users about the environmental benefits of carbon-neutral EV charging.

⚪️ Grid Modernization: Expand renewable energy penetration and adopt smart grid technologies.



⚡️ Future Projections ⚡️


⚪️ Renewable Energy-Powered Charging by 2030


India's commitment to renewable energy development positions it as a leader in sustainable EV charging. By 2030, it is estimated that 40-50% of EV charging stations across the country could be powered by renewable energy sources such as solar, wind, and biomass. This transition will be driven by the government's ambitious renewable energy targets, such as achieving 500 GW of non-fossil fuel capacity by the same year. Additionally, ongoing initiatives to integrate clean energy into the grid and advancements in distributed energy systems will play a critical role. The widespread deployment of renewable energy-powered EV chargers will not only reduce the carbon footprint of transportation but also enhance energy security and lower operational costs for EV owners.


⚪️ Preventing CO2 Emissions by 2040


Transitioning to carbon-neutral EV charging holds the potential to prevent 30-50 million tons of CO2 emissions annually by 2040. This significant reduction aligns with India's climate commitments under the Paris Agreement and its net-zero emissions goal by 2070. Achieving this milestone will require scaling up renewable energy integration into EV charging, expanding energy storage solutions, and deploying smart charging technologies. Moreover, carbon offsetting initiatives will address any residual emissions, ensuring the sustainability of the entire charging ecosystem.


The shift to carbon-neutral charging will also have a ripple effect across other sectors, promoting green jobs, fostering technological innovation, and enhancing public health by reducing air pollution. If pursued aggressively, these efforts could establish India as a global pioneer in decarbonizing transportation and achieving a sustainable future.
